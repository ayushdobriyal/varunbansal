Understanding Robotic Cardiac Surgery and its Components

Robotic cardiac surgery is a cutting-edge advancement in the field of cardiovascular medicine, employing state-of-the-art robotic systems to perform intricate cardiac procedures with precision. The primary components of a robotic system used in cardiac surgery include a console, robotic arms, and specialized instruments. The console allows the surgeon to control the robotic arms, which are equipped with miniaturized instruments that can navigate through smaller incisions compared to traditional surgery. .

This minimally invasive approach results in reduced pain, shorter recovery times, and enhanced precision, making robotic cardiac surgery a transformative option for patients requiring various heart procedures.

Robotic Cardiac Surgery

Robotic cardiac surgery represents a cutting-edge advancement in the field of cardiovascular medicine. This minimally invasive approach utilizes robotic systems to perform intricate cardiac procedures with unparalleled precision.

Minimally Invasive Cardiac Surgery

Minimally Invasive Cardiac Surgery (MICS) has revolutionized traditional approaches to heart procedures. In MICS, surgeons employ specialized techniques and instruments to perform surgeries through small incisions, often avoiding the need for a large chest opening.

Complex cardiac surgery

"Complex cardiac surgery involves intricate procedures designed to address intricate heart conditions. These surgeries are often necessary for complex cases, such as advanced heart valve disorders, congenital anomalies, or intricate coronary artery diseases.

Total arterial CABG

Total Arterial Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG) is an advanced surgical technique used to address coronary artery disease. In this procedure, surgeons exclusively use arterial grafts, typically the internal mammary arteries, instead of veins, to bypass blocked coronary arteries.

Mitral valve repair surgery

Mitral valve repair surgery is a specialized procedure aimed at correcting issues with the mitral valve, one of the heart's crucial valves. Rather than replacing the valve, surgeons focus on preserving and restoring its function.

Heart valve replacement surgery

Heart valve replacement surgery is a medical procedure performed to address damaged or diseased heart valves. During this surgery, the damaged valve is replaced with either a mechanical or biological prosthetic valve.

Heart transplant and LVADs

Heart transplantation and Left Ventricular Assist Devices (LVADs) represent advanced therapies for severe heart failure. In a heart transplant, a diseased heart is replaced with a healthy donor heart, offering a potential cure for end-stage heart failure. On the other hand, LVADs are mechanical devices implanted to assist the weakened heart in pumping blood.

Heart transplant

A heart transplant is a life-saving surgical procedure in which a diseased or failing heart is replaced with a healthy donor heart. This intricate medical intervention is typically reserved for individuals with severe heart conditions that cannot be effectively treated through other means.

TAVI

Transcatheter Aortic Valve Implantation (TAVI), also known as Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement (TAVR), is a revolutionary minimally invasive procedure for treating aortic valve stenosis.

Double valve replacements

Double valve replacement is a complex cardiac surgical procedure where both the aortic and mitral valves in the heart are replaced with artificial valves. This surgery is typically performed to address severe damage or disease affecting both valves simultaneously.


Redo – MVR

A Redo Mitral Valve Replacement (MVR) is a surgical procedure performed when a previously implanted mitral valve needs to be replaced again. This may be necessary due to wear and tear of the existing artificial valve, valve dysfunction, or other complications that have arisen since the initial surgery.

Pneumonectomy

Pneumonectomy is a surgical procedure involving the removal of an entire lung. Typically performed to treat severe lung diseases, lung cancer, or traumatic injuries, pneumonectomy aims to eliminate the affected lung tissue and improve overall respiratory function.
